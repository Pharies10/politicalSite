Foriegn Policy
Immigration:
  • We support humanitarian immigration policies in order to reunite families, but the primary goal of our immigration policy is to boost our intellectual capital
    • As of 2015, only 14% of immigrants obtain legal permanent residence for employment-related reasons; 44% gain residence because they have family members in the country already
    • We strongly oppose illegal immigration: every person must enter the U.S. through legal channels to prevent strains on our already overloaded economy

  • We propose a cap on the number of visas issued per year, in keeping with the RAISE Act as drafted in 2017
    • A minimum 30% of the total visas issued should be given to those with experience in top industries (technology, etc), not to exceed 50% of visas
    • Remaining visas to be issued to family members of immigrants and those with temporary employment which fluctuates (agricultural jobs, etc)

  • Visas will be issued according to a points-based system similar to the one in place in Canada
    • Priority will be given to those with skills to suit open positions in our growing technological sector, along with other STEM-related careers

  • We support strong border control, though we will not build a wall-we are trying to move past the mistakes of our predecessors
Enviroment
  • The Green New Deal is just another version of the “Year Zero” concept; it’s not actually about the environment at all.

  • In a study conducted globally to measure carbon emissions globally, the U.S. produces 16% of the world’s emissions

  • We propose to increase local consumption of goods produced agriculturally and on-site recycling
    • Similar to Marine Le Pen’s 2017 presidential manifesto for the FN (a.k.a. National Front) in France

  • We support an increase in laws regulating fertilizer use and crop varieties to lower environmental impact of importing goods from other countries
    • Soybeans in place of corn pull less nitrogen from soil that needs to be replenished by fertilizers
